Illustration &
Graphic Narrative

Visual Storytelling Through Illustration

This collection explores illustration as a visual storytelling medium, combining expressive imagery with graphic composition to communicate mood, narrative and atmosphere. The works span conceptual illustration, poster design and narrative imagery, demonstrating how illustration can function as both artistic expression and visual communication.

Through experimentation with colour, texture and composition, the projects investigate how illustrated imagery can evoke emotion, suggest narrative and create a distinctive visual identity. Each piece explores the relationship between illustration and design, where visual storytelling supports broader communication objectives.

Drama poster

Concept & Visual Expression

This illustration was developed as a conceptual drama poster, focusing on emotional intensity and psychological atmosphere. The composition centres on a solitary figure illuminated by dramatic lighting, creating a strong sense of tension and narrative ambiguity.

The visual language draws from theatrical poster traditions, where lighting, gesture and symbolic imagery work together to suggest a deeper narrative context. Painterly textures and controlled colour contrasts help emphasise the emotional tone of the piece while maintaining clarity in the overall composition.

The result is an illustration that communicates narrative through atmosphere rather than explicit storytelling, allowing the viewer to interpret the scene through visual cues.

Label Illustration Series

Illustration for Packaging Context

This series explores how illustration can function within product packaging and label design. Each piece was created to act as both a narrative artwork and a structured design element within a packaging system.

The illustrations combine expressive imagery with controlled framing, ensuring that visual storytelling complements the informational structure required in label design. Colour, scale and composition were carefully considered to maintain clarity when applied to packaging formats.

By balancing illustration with typographic hierarchy and layout structure, the work demonstrates how narrative imagery can enhance brand identity while supporting product communication.

Illustration book

Narrative Illustration

This project involved the development of illustrated pages for a children's picture book, focusing on character expression, environmental storytelling and narrative pacing.

The illustrations were designed to guide readers through the story visually, using composition and colour to create rhythm across the pages. Character gestures, spatial depth and lighting were carefully developed to reinforce the emotional tone of the narrative.

The final result presents a cohesive visual world where illustration, storytelling and layout work together to support an engaging reading experience.

From Sketch
to Final

Creative Development Process

This section presents the creative development behind the illustrations, revealing how early sketches evolve into finished visual works.

Initial drawings focus on composition, character placement and narrative direction, allowing ideas to be explored freely before moving into more refined illustration stages. During the development process, colour palettes, lighting and textures are gradually introduced to strengthen the visual atmosphere of the piece.

By presenting both sketches and final artwork, the process highlights how experimentation and iterative refinement contribute to the final visual outcome.
