Packaging System / Samaroli

Samaroli

A premium packaging system developed for one of the world’s most respected independent whisky bottlers. The project refines typographic hierarchy, label architecture and production detailing to create a more structured, scalable and collectible brand experience.

The Challenge

The task was to refine and systemise the label structure while preserving Samaroli’s heritage aesthetic. The design needed to improve clarity, hierarchy and scalability across multiple editions without losing the brand’s long-standing character and collectable appeal.

Design Process

The project moved from concept references and visual direction into label system development, print refinement and final brand application across physical and digital touchpoints.

Moodboard & Concept

Visual references rooted in heritage illustration and traditional whisky culture established the overall tone and narrative direction.

Label System

A structured framework was developed to create consistency across multiple releases while allowing room for variation.

Production Testing

Print finishes, colour reproduction and alignment accuracy were refined to ensure premium manufacturing quality.

Label System Development

A scalable label framework was created to define the relationship between brand, release and technical information. Typography, spacing and image composition were refined to build a strong hierarchy while keeping the distinctive Samaroli character intact.

Application Across Editions

The updated system was applied consistently across multiple releases, ensuring stronger continuity, improved visual recognition and a clearer structure for future editions.

Production & Material Strategy

Production detailing was used as part of the brand experience rather than decoration alone. Finishes such as foil, embossing and stock texture were considered carefully to reinforce Samaroli’s premium positioning.

Digital & Brand Extension

The packaging system extended beyond the bottle label into digital rollout, presentation materials and campaign assets, allowing the brand to maintain a unified identity across both physical and online touchpoints.

Before / After Evolution

The evolved system improved typographic hierarchy, spacing, alignment and production clarity while preserving the brand’s heritage and collectable tone.

At Samaroli, production detailing is not decorative — it is structural to the brand perception.
